Downhill Demesne & Mussenden Temple


Located along the north-western coast of Northern Ireland, Downhill started life effectively as a seaside holiday home, creating a convenient base for the Earl Bishop's volcanic explorations along the coast. Once ordained with statues and paintings, Downhill House held a beautiful collection of artwork which included Rembrandts, Raphaels, Titians and Tintorettos. Now, Downhill House still stands, but due to fires and years of deterioration, it has now all but abandoned.


During the Earl Bishop's time, Downhill House was noted for the richness of its decoration and the abundance of its works of art. Many of the rooms had elaborate ceilings; the walls were covered with fine plasterwork; and exquisite marble fireplaces adorned the living rooms.


Set along the cliffside, Mussenden Temple provides spectacular views, with Downhill Strand to the west, and Castlerock, Portstewart and Portrush to the east. Built in 1785, the temple was constructed as a summer library for the Earl Bishop, inspired by the Temple of Vesta in Tivoli, near Rome.
