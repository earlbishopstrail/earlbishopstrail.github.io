The Giant's Causeway


One of the most iconic landscapes in the world, the Giant's Causeway has inspired artists, poets and writers for centuries. The world famous basalt columns, formed through volcanic eruptions, are steeped in legend and irish forklore with stories of the mighty giant, Finn MacCool. Designated an Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty, the Giant's Causeway offers four stunning trails, with areas for hiking and picnic.


The Giant's Causeway Vistors Centre, a state-of-the-art complex built into the rising landscape, provides 360 degree views along the causeway coastline from its grass rooftop. Inside, visitors can experience its interactive spaces, discover the story of Finn MacCool on the big screen and taste seasonal, local produce from its café.


The Bishop of Derry, credited with bringing the Giant’s Causeway to the attention of an extensive audience was to be made a Fellow of the Royal Society in recognition of his scientific work. The Earl Bishop confirmed the existence of similar volcanic rocks on a visit to the Western Isles of Scotland giving some credence to the folktale that Finn MacCool had built the Causeway from Ireland to Scotland.


The volcanic origin of this entire section of the north coast so pleased the Earl Bishop that, when he acquired a portion of church land on a dramatic outcrop just west of Coleraine, he decided to build a house on it, Downhill House.
